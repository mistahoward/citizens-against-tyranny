A Firebrand from the Gravel Roads

Kai and Piper at the Joplin Public Library, May 2025.

Jess Piper is a former high school teacher turned political firebrand fighting for justice and democracy from the gravel roads of rural Missouri. She's not just advocating for public education—she's battling the deep-rooted corruption, culture wars, and corporate grift that plague red states like ours.

When she visited our local library, she didn't come with empty promises—she came to listen, to speak truth, and to remind us that democracy starts in rooms just like that. As host of The Dirt Road Democrat podcast, Piper champions teachers and exposes the con of school vouchers.

Jess Piper is the kind of leader rural America has been waiting for—unafraid, unapologetic, and unrelenting. Because tyranny doesn't just collapse—it gets pushed back, one rural dirt road at a time.

"I'm Not Going to Back Down"

Khennedi Meeks in San Jose, CA

The powerful words of 19-year-old Khennedi Meeks, spoken during a protest in the wake of the killing of George Floyd. Her stance is a testament to the courage of a new generation rising to confront injustice, reminding us that the fight for democracy is fought not just in hearing rooms, but on the streets.
